This video shows how to build stomach muscles if you are unable to stand, either temporarily or permanently. Despite this disability, there are still ways you can build your stomach muscles!

Exercising the core (center body) is crucial for people in wheelchairs because:

  1. Stability: A strong core provides stability while sitting, helping maintain an upright posture and preventing slumping.
  2. Balance: Core strength improves balance, reducing the risk of falls or accidents in the wheelchair.
  3. Functional Movement: A strong core supports reaching, transferring, and performing everyday tasks with greater ease.
  4. Prevention of Pain: Core exercises alleviate strain on the back, reducing the likelihood of discomfort or pain.
  5. Better Respiration: Strong core muscles aid in efficient breathing and lung function.
  6. Overall Strength: A solid core complements upper body strength, contributing to improved mobility and independence.

Incorporating center body exercises as a handicapped individual can significantly enhance your overall quality of life by offering a range of physical and functional benefits. These exercises are tailored to engage and strengthen the core muscles, which play a pivotal role in maintaining stability, balance, and posture. As your core becomes stronger, you'll likely experience improved ease in performing everyday tasks, enhanced mobility, and a heightened sense of confidence in your movements. This holistic approach to fitness can contribute to a more active and fulfilling lifestyle, promoting both physical well-being and a positive outlook on life.
